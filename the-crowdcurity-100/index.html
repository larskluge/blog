The CrowdCurity 100

Aug 28th, 2014

Over the past year, our business has grown from a handful of clients to a thriving security marketplace of researchers who have found and reported thousands of vulnerabilities to businesses. Today, we’re excited to share that CrowdCurity has hit an important milestone: to date, we have hosted over 100 bug bounty programs and crowdsourced security testing for businesses from all over the world. A year ago, we founded CrowdCurity with a mission: to help connect businesses and researchers, and to leverage the collective intelligence of the security community to help businesses of all sizes secure web applications for their users. security has become a high priority to companies across all sectors, especially because 2014 has been the year of the security breach. Since our launch:

  • CrowdCurity has hosted over 100 bug bounty programs

  • Our marketplace has grown into a community of more than 1500 security researchers

  • Businesses have patched and rewarded 1000 security vulnerabilities

  • And our highest reward given was $1500.

We have also seen massive bug bounty program adoption among Bitcoin companies, SaaS applications and by businesses in the Fintech sector. Our first clients, QueueIT, eConscribi, QuickBT, Justcoin and Vault of Satoshi were instrumental in helping us solve the challenges that arise from hosting bug bounty programs, they are all still crowdsourcing their security testing through CrowdCurity.

It has been a pleasure and a privilege to get to work with the first 100 companies who have crowdsourced their security testing in our marketplace, and we are thrilled to have hit the century mark. We are thankful for every single company who has signed up to run a bug bounty program in our marketplace, and we can’t wait to meet the next 100 companies who show that they take web application security seriously and run a bug bounty program.
