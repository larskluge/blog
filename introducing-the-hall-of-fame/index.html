Introducing the Hall of Fame

Jan 15th, 2014

Today, we’re excited to launch the CrowdCurity Hall of Fame, a place where we can recognize the best testers on our platform who help online businesses strengthen their security every day. While many testers have received monetary rewards and thank you messages from businesses across the world, we want to highlight the great efforts our testers have taken to make the web a safer place.

Cash Rewards vs. Hall of Fame

The “Hall of Fame” is a widely recognized way to highlight the contributions and efforts of testers in the bug bounty community. In some cases, businesses do not offer cash rewards, rather they only mention security testers in their respective Hall of Fame. At CrowdCurity, we believe that quality reward programs need both cash rewards and a hall of fame. The cash reward gives a tester motivation to take time to look deep into the application, while a hall of fame adds a competition element as well as the personal recognition skilled testers deserve.

CrowdCurity Tester Rankings On the CrowdCurity platform, all vulnerability feedback is given directly to testers by the businesses hosting bug bounty programs. Business feedback plays an important role in determining the overall score assigned to a security tester. Below are the current scores given based on vulnerability respectively. Over time, we will incrementally change tester scoring criteria to maintain a fair competition.

In addition to scoring vulnerability feedback, the businesses also give feedback on a scale of 1 to 5 on the quality of vulnerability reports. Overall, a high report quality score can increase a tester’s score up to 25%. Duplicates, Rejections and Out of Scope Duplicate vulnerabilities are a regular discussion topic in the security research community. Here is how we handle common vulnerability in the CrowdCurity Hall of Fame:

  • Duplicate vulnerabilities are given a positive score to recognize value of their work despite the issue being found by another tester.
  • Rejected vulnerabilities are given a neutral score.
  • Vulnerabilities assessed as “Out of Scope” negatively impact a tester’s score.
    Because these various vulnerability ratings can potentially negatively impact scores, it is important that testers understand the detail and scope of the bounty programs in which they participate.

Congratulations to the testers who are currently listed on the Hall of Fame-- we look forward to seeing who will join you in the future. Good luck!
