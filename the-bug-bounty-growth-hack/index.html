The Bug Bounty Growth Hack

Feb 17th, 2014

It is a small but clever growth hack: startups are launching bug bounty programs to promote and secure the sites and services they offer to users. To avoid the same fate as Snapchat and Kickstarter, a small group of technology companies are starting a trend that makes their security stronger, thwarting malicious attacks and surveillance programs.

Secret

Secret, an anonymous messaging app that has taken the Silicon Valley by storm, announced a bug bounty program that that resulted in instant media attention from Techcrunch and many others. Because Secret users rely on their anonymity to protect the intimate and private thoughts they share, securing the identity of its userbase is vital to the app’s success. By launching a bug bounty program that rewards researchers who discover security vulnerabilities in its mobile applications, Secret and its users can be confident that the platform is secure with valuable input and open communication from the security community.

Wickr

Wickr, a secure, encrypted messaging app, recently announced a $100,000 bug bounty reward to any researchers who could find and reproduce vulnerabilities that compromised the privacy of its users’ communications. By making the bug bounty reward so large, Wickr immediately grabbed headlines in the technology and security media, ensuring that researchers across the world would hear about their program. Security researchers participating in this bounty program may be motivated by the large reward to search for hard-to-find, time consuming vulnerabilities.

Tresorit

Tresorit, a filesharing service created by Hungarian startup, received immense positive attention from Venturebeat and Gigaom for launching a bug bounty program offering a $25,000 reward. At present, Tresorit is still actively promoting the bug bounty program on their website, as well as the fact that they have not been hacked since it went public.

For apps that provide privacy and anonymity to users, launching a bug bounty program is a sends a strong message of commitment to securing user data. These three cases illustrate a growing trend of technology startups are actively benefiting from their proactive approach to security by announcing their bug bounty programs to the world and inviting security researchers to strengthen their security.

Want to show your users that you are serious and proactive about your site security? Start your own bug bounty or responsible disclosure program today!
