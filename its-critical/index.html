It’s critical!

Dec 17th, 2013

When running a CrowdCurity reward program, businesses define reward sizes dependent on the criticality of a potential vulnerability. From a business perspective, the most interesting vulnerabilities are those which have a high impact on their business. To better align the interests of both security testers and businesses, our standard reward setup recommends reward sizes of $50, $300 and $1000 for a low, medium or high vulnerability, respectively.

When security testers submit a vulnerability, it is necessary that they determine its criticality. But how should a security tester define criticality of a bug? Below, we have outlined some general guidelines and examples of how to set criticality.

Low Vulnerabilities like insecure cookies, clickjacking or insufficient password complexity are generally of low criticality as they are dependent on other issues and cannot be exploited in itself. Additionally, the probability and consequences of these vulnerabilities are low.

Medium Cross-site request forgery (XSRF or CSRF) vulnerabilities or those that might result in the changing of user data are examples of medium criticality. These issues will be dependant on specific scenarios– for example, a tester demonstrating how a XSRF vulnerability could change the email of other users and be used to reset passwords– and may be likely to result in higher reward.

High Vulnerabilities of high criticality are those that would result in bypassing authentication. Again, these issues will depend on certain scenarios-- for example, a tester demonstrates a stored XSS vulnerability that can hijack administrator sessions on an otherwise secure site. A vulnerability like this would be likely to result in a high reward. Another example of a highly critical vulnerability is a successful SQL injection that could be used to delete users or bypass authentication.

Reporting Vulnerabilities In general, providing a Proof of Concept and steps to reproduce the vulnerability are required in order to receive a reward. When reporting vulnerabilities of any criticality, the following details in a Vulnerability Report will increase a tester’s chances of earning a reward:

  1. Detailed steps that can reproduce the vulnerability.
  2. Concise, easy-to-understand explanations of a vulnerability exploit.
  3. Proof of Concept.
  4. Providing attachments that help document the vulnerability.
