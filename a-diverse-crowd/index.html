A diverse crowd

Feb 8th, 2014

When we first launched our own bug bounty program in September 2013, we were all excited, curious, eager, and slightly anxious. We constantly monitored our server logs, regularly refreshing our backend statistics and Google Analytics every few minutes. Suddenly, the coolest began to happen: people from 20 different countries on 6 different continents began hacking and testing the CrowdCurity web application. In just a few months, we had created a truly global platform, a platform that has allowed us to tap into a truly global crowd of people passionate about information security.As of today, 5 months have passed and many reward programs are active and running on the CrowdCurity platform. Everyday, it is exciting to see talented security researchers sign up and contribute to making the web a safer place by submitting site vulnerabilities from all around the world.

To show the true strength in the vast numbers of researchers active on the CrowdCurity platform, our creative intern Kevin Descardes designed this visual graphic that illustrates the locations of our top security researchers from around the globe. So far, 32 different countries are represented, and we even have a security tester from French Polynesia!

The diversity in our marketplace of security testers emphasizes the core advantage of crowdsourcing web security. The threat from malicious hackers is global, self-distributed and creative, and that problem can’t be solved with corporate security consultants working locally and by the hour. What is needed to build a better, safer web is a creative and diverse crowd of security researchers motivated by the incentives that encourage them to find and patch the most critical flaws in web applications in the world, something that we at CrowdCurity are proud to be building.
