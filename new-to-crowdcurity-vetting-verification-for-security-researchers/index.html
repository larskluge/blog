Researcher Vetting + Verification

Aug 18th, 2014

Over the past few weeks, we’ve implemented a series of small changes and tweaks to the signup process for CrowdCurity testers. These changes are a step in building more trust between businesses and security researchers on our bug bounty program platform. Today, we want to give more insight the verification and vetting process that all security researchers go through before participating in bug bounty programs.

All security researchers are vetted on their skills and experience. Our vetting process is designed to ensure that our testers are the best and brightest crowd around, and takes into account a researcher’s skills, experience, and history of achievements in other bug bounty programs. This new step in the registration process ensures that only the best security researchers are active in CrowdCurity programs, and ensures that unreliable, unskilled testers do not slip through the registration process. All security researchers must undergo identity verification before receiving reward payments.

**To comply with anti-money laundering (AML) rules and regulations, we are required by law to keep a copy of valid identification for each security researcher who receive payment through our platform. We know what you’re thinking -- NO, we did not suddenly become the NSA! We have always asked for valid ID during the tester registration so that testers can be paid their bug bounty rewards as quickly as possible, but we are now taking extra steps to verify the identification we collect. We use Blockscore for ID verification through KYC (Know Your Customer) protocols, and we are committed to securely storing our tester data. We will never sell this information to a third party.

Profile Tips for Security Researchers Your CrowdCurity profile is an important tool in building trust with businesses hosting bug bounty programs. All testers are encouraged to complete their tester profiles, which are visible to businesses and could potentially be showcased in our Hall of Fame.

  • Include as much information as possible about your background and accomplishments in security and web application testing.
  • Complete your profile by adding social network profiles, a strong profile image and links to your work will speed up the vetting process.
  • We understand if you aren’t comfortable uploading your face on the internet-- but avoid using cartoons, and offensive or violent images for your avatar.
  • Users with incomplete profiles or inappropriate profile images may have to wait longer for the vetting process to be complete, or could be denied access to CrowdCurity.
    We’ve instituted these changes to give our researchers and businesses a positive experience on our platform. Do you have any questions or comments about these changes? Get in touch with us at hello@crowdcurity.com.
